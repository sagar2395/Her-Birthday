# 07 — Launch Strategy: Free vs. $15 (and what I'd actually do)

You floated two ways to spark word-of-mouth for the launch:

- **A) Free for a short window** (e.g. 1 month) so people post their gift and the buzz
  drives later sales.
- **B) $15 "on discount" for the first few weeks**, anchored against the real $30.

Both are good instincts. Here's the honest trade-off and the approach I'd recommend —
which is **mostly B, with free used as a precision tool, not a blanket offer.**

## Why blanket "free for everyone" is risky

| Problem with open free | Why it bites |
|---|---|
| **Attracts low-intent users** | "Free" pulls people who grab it and never finish or share — the opposite of the engaged sharers you want. |
| **Zero willingness-to-pay signal** | Your #1 unknown is *will people pay $30?* A free launch teaches you nothing about that — you could "succeed" and still have no business. |
| **Anchors value at $0** | Once a market sees it free, converting them (or their friends) to $30 is much harder. You can't un-ring that bell. |
| **You eat cost + moderation with no revenue** | Each free app still costs storage + carries abuse/moderation risk, with nothing offsetting it. |
| **Sharing isn't guaranteed** | Free doesn't *make* people post. The thing that makes people post is an emotional reaction worth filming — which you engineer, not buy with a $0 price. |

Free *feels* like it buys word-of-mouth, but it mostly buys volume of the wrong kind.

## Why the $15 early-bird is the stronger public offer

- **It still validates payment** — the one thing you must learn before building more.
- **It filters for real intent** — people who pay $15 are far likelier to finish, love
  it, and post it.
- **It anchors the real price** — "$15, normally $30" makes $30 feel fair later, and
  creates urgency ("founding price — first 100 buyers / this week only").
- **Margin is still positive** — even at $15 you net ~$13 after costs
  ([05-earnings-model.md](./05-earnings-model.md)), so early sales fund themselves.

## The better word-of-mouth engine: free as *targeted seeding*, not a public price

Use free deliberately, where it actually creates great content:

1. **Founding gifts (≈10–25, hand-picked).** Give it free to a small set of
   *emotionally compelling or high-reach* people — couples, family/relationship
   micro-creators, a friend with a genuinely moving story — **in exchange for a
   reaction video + permission to repost.** This manufactures the cry-moment clips that
   *are* your ads (see [06-first-customers.md](./06-first-customers.md)), with quality
   control. This is influencer seeding, not a giveaway.
2. **A referral reward, not a free-for-all.** Give every buyer a code: their friend gets
   **$5 off**, they get a **renewal credit**. This engineers sharing while keeping price
   integrity intact.
3. **The "Made with ❤️ on [brand]" footer** already turns every published gift into a
   tiny billboard — free virality on each *paid* sale.

## Recommendation

> **Public launch at $15 "founding price" (normally $30), framed as limited
> (first N buyers / two weeks).** Separately, hand-give ~10–25 **free founding gifts** to
> high-reach/high-emotion people for reaction videos + repost rights, and ship a
> **referral discount** from day one. Raise to $30 once the founding window closes and the
> testimonials are rolling in.

This gets you the word-of-mouth of "free" (via targeted seeding + the footer + referrals)
**and** the payment validation + price integrity of a real launch. It's the best of both
ideas without the downside of teaching the market your product is worth nothing.

> One nuance to test: you can also split "free" cleverly — the *app* is paid, but offer
> **year-1 hosting free** (it nearly is anyway). "Free hosting" reads as generous without
> discounting the product itself.
