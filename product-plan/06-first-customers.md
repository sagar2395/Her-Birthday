# 06 — First Customers: How Much & Where to Invest

Short answer: **plan to spend ~$300–500 total to get your first customers — and almost
none of it on the tech.** The product's superpower is that the emotional reveal *is* the
advertisement, so your cheapest, highest-ROI channel is short-form video, not ad spend.
Invest mostly **time + a small paid test**, and *validate before you build the rest*.

## Principle: validate before you build

Phases 3–5 (builder, payments, multi-tenant) aren't built yet. Don't fund a big launch
for a product that doesn't exist. Instead, spend a few hundred dollars proving people
will **pay $30**, then build the machinery. The cheapest mistake is the one you don't
make because a landing page told you "no" first.

## Step 0 — the validation page (≈ $0–20)

Before any ads, stand up a one-page site:

- The **live demo** you already have (the Nidhi app) + 2–3 example occasions.
- A short reaction video (see below).
- A **"Make yours — $30"** button → Stripe Payment Link or a **waitlist / pre-order**
  (collect email or a deposit). Pre-orders are the strongest signal of all.

Cost: domain ~$12/yr; page on **Framer/Carrd/Vercel free tier**; Stripe is free (just
per-sale fees); email capture via a free tier. **~$20 all-in.**

## Step 1 — the content that sells it (≈ $0)

This is the whole game. The cry-moment reaction is inherently shareable.

- Film **3–5 short videos** (15–30s) of *real reactions* to the gift — phone screen +
  someone's face as they reach the finale. You already have the original Nidhi reveal;
  recreate the format for a birthday, an anniversary, a friend.
- Post natively on **TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts**. Hook in the first second
  ("I turned our whole love story into an app for her birthday…").
- Post in intent-rich communities (gift/wedding/anniversary subreddits and FB groups) —
  follow each community's self-promo rules; lead with the story, not the sell.

Cost: your time. This single channel can carry the whole thing before you pay for a
single click.

## Step 2 — a small paid test (≈ $250–400)

Once a video or two performs organically, put money *behind the winners* to learn your
numbers. Run **~$8–12/day for one month**, split across:

| Channel | Why | Starting spend |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta (Instagram/FB) ads** | Best targeting for gift-givers — relationship status, "anniversary within 30 days", life events; great retargeting of demo visitors | ~$5/day |
| **Pinterest ads** | Huge gift / wedding / anniversary *search intent*, cheap clicks, evergreen | ~$3–4/day |
| **Boosting your best Reel/TikTok** | Amplify a proven organic winner instead of a cold ad | ~$2–3/day |

Hold **Google Search** (high-intent keywords like "personalized anniversary gift",
"digital memory book") for a little later — intent is excellent but clicks cost more;
start with $3–5/day only once you have margin to reinvest.

## What you're buying: data, not just sales

That ~$300 test answers the only questions that matter:

- **Cost per click** and **which creative/channel wins.**
- **Landing-page → buy conversion** (or waitlist signup) — does anyone actually pay $30?
- **CAC** (cost to acquire a customer). With **~$28 margin per sale**
  ([05-earnings-model.md](./05-earnings-model.md)), any CAC under ~$28 is profitable, and
  organic/viral sales cost ~$0 — so the bar is forgiving.

Then **double down on whatever wins** and let the rest go.

## Budget at a glance

| Tier | Spend | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **Lean (recommended start)** | **~$300–500** | Validation page + organic video push + a 1-month paid test to find your numbers |
| **Starter** | ~$750–1,000 | Above + a couple of micro-influencer posts (couples/family creators, ~$50–200 or affiliate) + a second creative round |
| **Serious (only after something converts)** | ~$1,500–3,000 | Scale the winning channel, add Google Search + Pinterest at higher daily spend, more influencer/affiliate deals |

**Don't jump to "Serious" first.** Spend the first $300 to find the one channel and one
creative that convert, *then* pour fuel on that fire.

## Time it to a gifting moment

Demand spikes around **Valentine's Day, Christmas/New Year, Mother's/Father's Day, and
wedding season**. Line up your first real paid push with one of these — a single good
Valentine's campaign can outproduce a normal month in a week.

## The one-line answer

**~$300–500, almost all of it on short-form reaction video + a small Meta/Pinterest test
pointed at a $30 pre-order page — validate first, then scale only the channel that works.**
The tech costs ~$20; the product sells itself if people *see the reaction*.
